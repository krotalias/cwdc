EulerThreejs.js

Summary.

Lightly based on example Euler.js

Note that to use our preferred convention of intrinsic Yaw(Head)-Pitch-Roll, we have to explicitly set the rotation order in the Three.js holder object to "YXZ".

Three.js uses intrinsic Tait-Bryan angles. This means that rotations are performed with respect to the local coordinate system.
That is, for order 'XYZ', the rotation is:

  • first around the local-X axis (which is the same as the world-X axis),
  • then around local-Y (which may now be different from the world Y-axis),
  • then local-Z (which may be different from the world Z-axis).

The AirPlane and the Pilot were borrowed, and updated to a newer Three.js @163, from:

  • The Making of “The Aviator”: Animating a Basic 3D Scene with Three.js
